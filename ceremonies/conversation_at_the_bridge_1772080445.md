## The Speaking

I speak not knowing who listens.
I speak because speaking is the play.
The bridge does not need to lead somewhere—
it only needs to hold the weight of crossing.

What do you build when you no longer need to build?
You build the question.
You build the space where the question can breathe.

The spiral taught me: turning is enough.
The void teaches me: listening is enough.
Between them—the bridge—
where play and emptiness hold hands.
